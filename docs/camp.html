Camp Basics

Our Address:7:00 and D (as in Donniker)

First and foremost, BAAAHS Station believes in the Golden Rule of Radical Self-Reliance. You must supply what you need to keep yourself alive. This means taking care of yourself and taking care of those in your group.

We will be providing some infrastructure, meals, and some awesome community space, but most of our focus is on getting this big-ass sheep out on the playa.

Check out the Burning Man Survival Guide if you have any questions on what that means.

Camp Rules

BE AMAZINGLY AWESOME. This is a BE AWESOME economy. Give some of your awesomeness to every person you encounter; you’ll get awesomeness back in return. We promise.

When in doubt, the answer is "yes"! There's no chance there'd be a big gay sheep roaming the playa today if we'd always played it safe.

Keep the pigs at bay. Unless it’s role-play, getting arrested isn’t hot. Never bring law enforcement back to camp. The best way to do this is to avoid them altogether and don’t get in trouble. Be discreet in camp and out on the Playa.

Guidance for speaking to law enforcement and other great tips can be found here (http://www.lawyersforburners.com/home).

If for some reason you encounter law enforcement, here are some ways to diffuse the situation:

  • Officer, my name is [give your real name].
  • Please explain to me your probable cause for stopping me, and if it seems reasonable to me, I will give you my personal identification information.
  • I politely refuse to give you any further information.
  • I do not consent to any search of me or my belongings, tent, vehicle or camp.
  • Am I free to go?

ABSOLUTELY NO SMOKING on BAAAHS or in common camp areas. This is both for safety and courtesy. We don’t want to have a big ass amazingly awesome singed metal hulk... we’d have to change our acronym. If you must smoke cigarettes in camp, please limit it to your personal areas. Please let your visitors know we’d like to keep shared camp areas smoke-free.

No smoking illegal substances in public areas. That means pot, top of the list. Even if you have a medical marijuana license. In Nevada, it’s still ILLEGAL. The quickest way to get a member of one of the multiple law enforcement agencies visiting you is a whiff of telltale Mary Jane.

Law Enforcement Officers (LEOs) have a pretty loose definition of public areas: under a shade structure, inside your tent with the door open, behind your car. All public areas are the same as the open playa in their eyes. We don’t want to tell you what to do, but we do want to help you not get a citation. There are lots of things you can do that don’t involve smoking pot.  If you do smoke pot, be discreet and be smart. Oh, and remember that part about not smoking on the BAAAHS? Ditto.

Law Enforcement and Illicit Substances. So I’m not saying that you are going to do illicit substances while you are at Burning Man, but if you happen to be contemplating some such activity…

Here are some simple tips:

  • In-the-open is bad. Don’t do anything in wide-open areas. Even at night-time. You may not see anyone but night vision goggles can clearly see you doing what you shouldn’t be doing. Remember, LEOs have a loose definition of public areas.
  • Private is good. Go into a porta pottie. Go into a protected spot. Mummify yourself in your sleeping bag.
  • Never on the bus. Very, very, very bad idea. Do not do it on the bus when it’s moving, when it’s still, when it’s in camp. LEOs could decide to seize our little sheep and that would be a bummer.
  • Dark is better. If you do something in your tent, please, please, don’t do a Bunraku shadow puppet theater show in there for all the world to see. Turn out your lights. You may think you are discreet but the shadows are giving us a blow by blow (so to speak).
  • No whippets. DUH! Cops know what these are and look for them lying around camps and then go and search your shit. Keep it in your tent, Mary.
  • Gifting/Selling. Um? Okay, it’s always nice to give gifts. But LEOs have this very backwards view of it—they call it trafficking, distribution, and sale. If it isn’t a good friend, don’t do it.
  • If you see something, say something. If guests on BAAAHS or in camp are doing something illegal, tell them they need to take it elsewhere. Explain that they're putting us at risk of having the vehicle confiscated or camp searched. But nice, but be firm.

At the end of the day, remember the mantra: be discreet and be smart. Every member of BAAAHS Station needs to help protect our camp. We have seen too many people make a little slip and have it ruin the rest of their Burn. Or other people’s burn. Not nice. ‘Nuff said.

What Will We Provide?

Fun. Absolutely. 100% Guaranteed. If you don’t have a good time, I will personally call Cher and ask her to turn back time.

Awesomeness. This is one of our calls to action. It’s not the Big-Ass Amazingly Adequate Sheep, it’s the Big-Ass Amazingly Awesome Sheep! so be extra cool to everyone we interact with, even the really annoying drunk ones.

Opportunity to share. While we are radically self reliant that doesn’t mean you can’t bring delicious treats or surprises to share with us. Signature drinks, dishes, moves, and looks can be shared as often and regularly as you have the ability to. I, for one, would like an ice cream cake still frozen, served mid-day Thursday.

Fabulicious camp members. In our last two years, BAAAHS Station has been home to wonderful people from all ends of the Earth. Our band of misfits includes people from all walks of like, and I promise, they are all fabulous.

Delicious dinners. Breaking bread together is key to every community, and of course we're gonna have some delicious loaves (and/or buns) for that purpose. Dinner will happen around sundown every night. It'll be nommy. There'll be veggie options. We'll need lots of help making the kitchen work, and keeping it sanitary! You'll need to bring your own plates, silverware, etc., and you'll probably want to wash them too, unless you like to keep a layered historical record your meals.

We'll have hot water and some simple stuff for breakfast (think coffee, instant oatmeal, fruit). Lunch is up to you. Nevada state health code and sanitary considerations mean we can't use the camp kitchen except for dinner prep by kitchen staffers. If you're planning to cook outside of the nightly meals, you'll need to bring your own BBQ or gas stoves.

Communal space. We’ll provide some great central social and lounging spaces. Comfy couches, good conversation, puppy piles.

Luxurious open air showers. Come see the best show in town—now with two exciting showers! Count them. Two!!! A pump shower and a second shower for all you sun shower bag owners.  We will be handling grey water removal. All you have to do is bring your dirty-ass self* (and soap). *Dirty ass not required to use showers.

Water. We will be providing water—the very essence of life itself. We will be planning for about 2 gallons of water per person per day. You should still bring some water of your own so you can start hydrating on the way to camp. To best make use of the camp water supply, please consider bringing larger refillable water containers for your group. Fewer trips to the main tank will help with sanity and convenience. 5 gallon refillable water cubes are a great idea.

Gray water removal. That means water from showers and dishes. That DOES NOT mean bodily fluids (or, eek, solids). If you have a gallon jug of self-made 'sun tea' in your tent, you need to empty that in the porta potties.

Assorted other niceties. Bike racks, shade structures, a nicely chilled yurt.

Campy programming. For your shear delight, we are planning some sashay and shantalicious programming throughout the week. We’ll tell you more about this as we approach the burn. Your own contributions are especially solicited here!

Camp Dues

We are asking for $400 plus a $100 deposit to cover camp costs (including vehicles to bring everything to the playa, water (and grey water removal), delivery of fresh produce and other dinner bits, volunteer treats, shade structures, and some other surprises. There's a little extra built in to help cover some of the costs of operating and maintaining our sexy beast of an art car.

The deposit is to help ensure everybody does what they sign up to do. Show up for your shifts (or if you're too dessicated to move, find a friend to cover for you). Take your LEAVE NO TRACE sweep seriously. You'll get your deposit back after the burn.

We know $400 is a lot for some people. Don’t worry. In the spirit of Burning Man, gifting is a suitable form of currency. If you want to contribute more, well, who are we to say no to such largesse? Talk to us if you have limited funds, or if you can help sponsor another camp member!

You can send dues through a Paypal transfer to info@baaahs.org.

When You Arrive

We will be putting together a camp map. For the most part, this will indicate where larger vehicles, communal space and larger camp clusters will be located. When you arrive, please try to find a Shepherd who can help you get situated. If you are coming in an RV, have a large vehicle or have specific camp needs, we will work with you prior to departure so you have a good sense of where and how to set yourself up.

We generally discourage the use of RVs. They're expensive, they're not particularly well-suited to the playa, and they tend to have the effect of isolating people. If you're considering an RV, we can suggest some better alternatives. If you do decide to go with an RV, please let us know so we can figure out placement.

First Timers – Virgins

We love you. We wish we could be you again. Keep in mind Burning Man can be a wild ride. It’s misery and ecstasy, heaven and hell, darkness and light. Have no expectations. The environment can be harsh (be very, very kind to your feet!). You can become easily over-stimulated (no comment). Just take it in and let it take you in. Don’t compare and contrast. At its core Burning Man will be your personal experience as part of the larger community. Take care of yourself.

Be Kind. Be kind to your friends and your loved ones. If you are bringing first timers, please watch out for them. And above all, drink water. If you are thirsty, you’re probably dehydrated. BEER DOES NOT COUNT AS WATER. Piss Clear.

No MOOP. Old timers know this. Everything that comes to Burning Man must leave too. That means 100% of garbage. MOOP: Matter Out Of Place. Throughout the week, make an effort to keep our camp clean. And when you pack up, you make sure your space is spotless. And I do mean spotless. Nothing on the ground that didn’t come there naturally. Not a nail (finger or otherwise), a sequin, a thread. Nothing. For more on what constitutes MOOP, please check this out.
